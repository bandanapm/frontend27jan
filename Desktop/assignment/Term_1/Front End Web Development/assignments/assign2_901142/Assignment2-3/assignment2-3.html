National Traditions and Customs of Nepal

Dashain
A major Hindu festival celebrated in Nepal, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. It spans over 15 days and involves worship, family gatherings, feasting, and the flying of kites.
Tihar
Also known as Deepawali or the Festival of Lights, Tihar is a five-day festival celebrated in Nepal. It honors various aspects of life, including the relationship between humans and animals. People light oil lamps, decorate their homes, perform rituals, and exchange blessings and gifts.
Buddha Jayanti
Also called Vesak or Buddha Purnima, it is a significant Buddhist festival in Nepal. It commemorates the birth, enlightenment, and death of Lord Buddha. Buddhists engage in prayer, meditation, and visit monasteries during this day.
Gurkha Tradition
The Gurkhas are renowned soldiers from Nepal who have served in the British and Indian armies. The Gurkha tradition includes their distinctive military service, bravery, and the recruitment of young men from Nepal into Gurkha regiments.
Newari Culture
Newars are an indigenous community in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal. They have a rich cultural heritage, known for their unique art, architecture, festivals, music, dance, and cuisine. Newari traditions and customs reflect the valley's history and cultural diversity.
Indra Jatra
Indra Jatra is a vibrant Newari festival celebrated in Kathmandu. It is dedicated to Lord Indra, the king of heaven, and the rain god. The festival involves chariot processions, masked dances (Lakhe Naach), the display of the deity's idol, and the erection of a wooden ceremonial pole (lingo) in Kathmandu Durbar Square.
Yomari Punhi
Yomari Punhi is a Newari festival celebrated to mark the end of the rice harvest season. It involves the preparation and consumption of a special delicacy called Yomari. Yomari is a rice flour dough filled with sweet substances like molasses, sesame, or sugar, and shaped like a fish or a crescent moon.
Mha Puja
Mha Puja, meaning "self-worship," is a Newari festival celebrated during the New Year according to the lunar calendar. It involves a special ceremony where individuals worship and honor their own bodies. It is believed to bring good health, longevity, and prosperity.
Holi
Holi is a colorful festival celebrated by Hindus in Nepal. It marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. Participants throw vibrant colored powders and water at each other, sing and dance, and indulge in festive treats.
Maghe Sankranti
Maghe Sankranti is a traditional harvest festival celebrated in Nepal. It falls on the first day of the month of Magh in the Nepali calendar. People take holy dips in rivers, visit temples, fly kites, and consume traditional dishes like ghee, molasses, and sesame-based sweets.
Lhosar
Lhosar is a festival celebrated by different ethnic communities, especially the Tamang and Sherpa communities in Nepal. It marks the Tibetan New Year and involves dancing, singing, feasting, and the performance of traditional rituals.
